People ask about my setup occasionally. Here's what I'm actually using day-to-day in 2025 — no affiliate links, just honest picks.

<!-- more -->

## Editor

**VS Code** with:
- `vim` keybindings (no going back once you learn them)
- GitHub Copilot — genuinely useful for boilerplate and tests, not for core logic
- Catppuccin Mocha theme — easy on the eyes for long sessions

## Terminal

**Windows Terminal** + **PowerShell 7** on Windows, **iTerm2** + **zsh** on Mac.

`oh-my-zsh` with just three plugins:
- `git` — alias shortcuts
- `z` — jump to frequently used directories
- `syntax-highlighting` — shows errors before you hit Enter

## Browser DevTools

I spend way too much time in Chrome DevTools. The most underused panels:
- **Performance** tab — record and analyze frame drops
- **Network** tab with "Disable cache" always on during dev
- **Coverage** tab — find unused JS and CSS

## Note-Taking

**Obsidian** with local files (no cloud sync). The graph view is mostly a toy but the linking between notes is genuinely useful for connecting ideas across topics.

## Learning

I maintain a private reading list in Obsidian. When I encounter a concept I don't understand, I add it. Weekly review, pick one, go deep.

The biggest productivity gain I've found: **one deep focus session per day, everything else is admin**. Two hours of real concentration beats eight hours of fragmented work.
